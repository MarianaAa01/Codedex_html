⟢ ₊ ⊹ Welcome

  • What's a cat cafe?

    • Cat cafes originated in Taiwan, with one opening in Taipei in 1998.
    The cafe became popular with Japanese tourists as well as local visitors, who enjoyed the opportunity to interact with some furry friends.
    One of the Japanese tourists took the idea back to Japan, and the first Japanese cat cafe opened its doors in Osaka in 2004.
    They’ve become very popular in Japan, with Tokyo home to at least 39.

  • How it works

    • We know that many of you may have cats at home, but things work differently at The Cat Café.
    Since we are working with rescued cats and want to make sure they are treated well, we ask our patrons to do the following:

    1. Be respectful of the animals and learn the signs they show when they want you to ease back.
      We have posted signs describing the way cats show discomfort with their tails and their ears. Please keep these in mind when interacting with the cats.

    2. Don’t pick up the cats. Most cats don’t like to be held, and they are not shy about letting you know this.
      We don’t want anyone to get scratched or bitten by a cat who’s not happy about being picked up.

    3. Cats don’t like loud noises, so please keep your voices down when you enter the playpen. We have a sound monitor in the cat playpen.
      A siren will let us know when it gets too loud. Please remember to keep the volume level down so we don’t have to hear that siren.

    4. Photography is fine — the internet is all about cat pictures and videos!
      However, flash photography disturbs the cats, so please refrain from that.

    5. The Cat Café has a zero tolerance policy for any individual who is causing distress to the animals.
      It’s a rare occasion for us to have to ask someone to leave. We’d like to keep it that way.

  • Benefits to Cats

    • There are many benefits for cats. Here are some of them:

    1. Fostering saves the lives of millions of cats.
      By freeing up space in shelters, additional cats can be taken in and more animals will be saved.

    2. Fostering allows animals that require special attention to get it.

    3. The environment at The Cat Cafe gives our feline guests a chance to feel at home.

    4. Potential adopters will be able to learn more about the personality of the cats.
